Beaches

Winnifred Beach

Perched like a baby bird on a cliff 13km east of Port Antonio you’ll find the little district of Fairy Hill and a rugged dirt track. Follow that road steeply downhill and you’ll reach Winnifred Beach, yet another totally gorgeous beach that puts a lot of the sand in more famous places (ahem: MoBay, Negril) to shame. It’s the only truly public beach on this stretch of the coast, and has a great vibe, with FOOD AND DRINK stands, weekend sound systems and Jamaicans from all walks of life.

Frenchman’s Cove Beach

This little cove just east of Drapers boasts a small but perfect white-sand beach, where the water is fed by a freshwater river that spits directly into the ocean. The area is still technically owned by the Frenchman’s Cove Resort. There’s a snack bar serving jerk chicken and fish, bike rental (US$20 a day), alfresco showers, bathrooms, a secure parking lot and the option of taking boat tours to the Blue Lagoon (US$20). Look for the entrance opposite the San San Golf Course.

Boston Bay Beach

One of the few destination in the world where beaches have remained completely unspoiled. A destination that unlocks the mystery of nature – an oasis with amazing sunsets, oceanic views and hot spots which are an ideal escape for island lovers.

Long Bay Beach

Further east along the coast is the beautiful beach and district of Long Bay. There are bars and ‘chillin’ spots along the white sandy beach dotted with low coconut palms. A bucolic spot.

San San Beach

San San is another gorgeous private beach used by residents of the villas on Alligator Head, and guests of the Goblin Hill, Fern Hill and Jamaica Palace hotels. The bay is enclosed by a reef that’s wonderful for snorkeling (US$10 per day) and kayaking (US$25 per hour). Undeveloped Monkey Island (there are no monkeys – damn) is a good snorkel spot, and you can swim here from the beach if you’re in decent shape.
