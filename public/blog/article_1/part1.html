FINDING SINOPIA INN IS EASY

Finding Sinopia Inn is easy. However if you drive to fast along Portland's main road you’ll miss the turn into Zion Hill. Here you’ll find a newly renovated country home. It was first built in the early 20th century as a country home and rebuilt in the same spirit – nestling on the slopes of Zion Hill, with a contemporary interior decor that compliments the parishes natural rustic charm. Here you can expect tranquil nights and alternative accommodation to the resort locations of Montego Bay, Ocho Rios and Negril.

On entry you’ll find a range of spots for solitude. A dark reddish-brown main villa whose vibrancy is brought to life by the vast array of colors set on two acres of lush green gardens, filled with palm trees and plants making it a stunning hideaway.


Outside you’ll notice the striking floor tiles leading up to the main villa. On your left you have an outdoor lounge for breakfast, wine, cocktails and conversations supported by bamboo columns with a mahogany railings separating you from the edge of the cliff, with a doorway that opens into a spacious air conditioned living / dinging area ; adjoining one of the air conditioned bedrooms filled with both traditional and modern furniture of West Indian design ; bright patterned floor tiles and ceiling work that invokes a feeling of going back in time as the wooden beams used to support the roof have remained intact for centuries.

On your right is the villa’s verandah that has retained its original features and is shaded from the sun, providing cool seating that overlooks the colours of the landscape and tropical garden. The original wooden flooring wraps around the entire building joining both wings. Ending with a stairway leading to a lush green lawn and neighbouring olive green wooden gate, reinforced by dark redish brown brickwork that seamlessly merges with the building and its interior.


If you choose to venture through these gates don't expect to find grand and imposing historic architecture built by tycoons of the era. Here you'll find mountain side filled with aspiring developments of a much smaller vernacular style.

But lets back track are steps for a minute and find another one the the many spots for solitude and entertaining. The outdoor gazebo purposely built for welcoming and entertaining guest outdoors on the grounds leads up to a man made jerk pit. Incased in bamboo stilts and fuelled by cedar wood the design and style humbly respects the islands culture.

In a more secluded part of the grounds you’ll see a another country house where little has changed since in was built. Its facades and windows still remains intact for centuries and has survived the infamous hurricane Gilbert of 1988. Today this house gives promise to more developments and an experience that most should come to know and love as Sinopia Inn’s setting creates a relaxed environment with rustic views , complemented by personalised services and accommodation that is ideal for hip travellers, family, friends, loved ones and acquaintances.That will have you feeling the vibes to just chill.
