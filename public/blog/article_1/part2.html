FINDING SINOPIA INN IS EASY

Soon after arriving on the direct British Airways flight from London, England’s Gatwick Airport, into Kingston’s Norman International Airport, I was out in the sun and on my way. I was pleasantly surprised by the combination of the sun beating down and the cool breeze blowing over the Caribbean Sea as I rattled along the empty coast road into the city.

During my visit through the months of October and November Jamaica’s rich history and local culture shone bright. Every visitor to the island can expect to taste the best in Jamaican cooking as chefs from every corner of the island came out to showcase their culinary skills at the island’s annual Food and Drink Festival and Restaurant Week.


But what to expect? Definitely rum, roots and reggae - all of the things Jamaica is known for but while I waited for the annual Restaurant Week to kick off, I was able to discover so much more throughout the city. New bands, music venues, festivals and events.

A great place to go is Dub Club, an amazing cultural platform for emerging artists hosted by Gabriele Selassie, who recalls that after speaking with his good friend and international reggae superstar Luciano, he decided to launch Dub Club to deliver a platform for reggae music all the year round - and not just at the grand staged events of Reggae Sumfest and Rebel Salute.


Entrance is free throughout the week from 12:00 to 10:00pm except on Sunday when Gabriele Selassie invites artists to perform or wows the crowd with a all night long juggling session of rocking reggae beats in the cool hills above Kingston overlooking the amazing skyline. Here some of my oldest friends have featured as they have journeyed on to international reggae stardom. During this trip to Jamaica I was lucky enough to sit down with Kabaka Pyramid who shared his most recent travel experience with me.
