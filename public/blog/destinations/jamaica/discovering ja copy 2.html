DISCOVERING JA

Being the son of a Jamaican lecturer and Facilities Manager, I was fortunate to spend some of my best years, so far, living in Jamaica. Now I spend my time travelling back and forth, between London and Jamaica, working on my dream of owning a small guest house with a nice restaurant that echoes the charm of the island.

When I arrived during the month of October, I immediately checked into a small hotel fitted with a contemporary decor and art that showcases the work of Jamaican artists.

After enjoying time to relax I head to Scotchies to enjoy traditional Jamaican speciaslity of jerk, rice and beans, washed down with an ice cold coconut water, followed by a Red Stripe beer.

But what a gwann?! By the time I left Scotchies, it was six in the evening and Kingston’s exciting nightlife was just beginning. Whether it was a local tournament or a vibing street party, I made sure I found time to enjoy some the city’s best hotspots as they come alive with reggae, jazz music and rum.

During this trip I spent a lot of time in Kingston City working on this project talking to people about the island, constantly reminding myself of the history, politics and culture. Hoping to get my creative juices flowing I connected with Duane Bennett and sister Teasea Bennett. When talking about their brand Rêve Jewellery & Accessories, they are very passionate. They talk about being, “Fuelled by a genuine enthusiasm and passion for our heritage…the beautiful island, people, culture, music and even the cuisine of JAMAICA all serve as the source of constant inspiration to this authentic JAMAICAN made line.” Their words resonate with me to this day - as I strongly believe that creativity comes from tradition and heritage with a modern twist.

Bumping into these two genuine people while shopping in Devon House seemed like fate. Both born in Jamaica, they use the island’s rich natural resources to incorporate the best quality precious, semi-precious and natural stones, beads and metals including non-changing brass, bronze, copper, sterling silver (rhodium and nickel free) and gold into each fashion piece. This is the same philosophy I use when thinking of a great dish!

On August 8th, the pair host an annual wellness festival that showcases their pieces and a day filled with activities for children, cooking demonstrations and fashion show presenting the top Jamaican and Caribbean designer brands. They can be found in Devon House 26 Hope Road | Shop#13 Devon House Courtyard, Kingston KGN 10, Jamaica.
