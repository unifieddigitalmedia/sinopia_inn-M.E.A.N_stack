THE WEEKENDER

On entry you’ll find a range of spots for solitude. A red-ochre main villa whose vibrancy is brought to life by the vast array of colors set on two acres of lush green gardens, filled with palm trees and variegated plants that make it a stunning hideaway.

But let’s back track our steps for a minute and find another one of the many spots for solitude and entertaining. The outdoor gazebo, purpose-built for welcoming and entertaining guest outdoors on the grounds, leads up to a custom-made jerk pit. Encased in bamboo stilts and fueled by cedar wood, the design and style respects the island’s culture.

In a more secluded part of the grounds you’ll see another country house where little has changed since it was built. Its facade and windows still remain intact having survived the devastating Hurricane Gilbert of 1988. Today this house gives promise of more developments and an experience that most should come to know and love. This is because Sinopia Inn’s setting creates a relaxed environment with rustic views, complemented by personalised services and accommodation perfect all year. Ideal for weekend breaks and winter getaways. It is an easy escape into the cool hills of the island’s countryside. It will have you feeling the vibes to just…chill.


WHAT IS IT

A country home first built in the early 20th century and rebuilt in the same spirit – nestling on the slopes of Zion Hill, with a contemporary interior decor that complements the parish’s natural rustic charm. Here you can expect tranquil nights and alternative accommodation to the resort locations of Montego Bay, Ocho Rios and Negril. The villa is fully self-catered meaning it’s your home for the weekend. With no disturbances or interruptions as the proprietors do not live onsite.


BEHIND THE SCENES

Set on 2 acres Sinopia Inn was once home to the Beckford’s and is now owned by their decedents.


SLEEP

Three of the four beautifully rebuilt bedrooms feature ensuite bathrooms with double beds and the fourth features two double beds with added lounge area and ensuite bathroom.


EAT

Guests can talk through plans with the chef who's up early to prepare a traditional Jamaican breakfast.


WHO GOES THERE

Millennials wanting to enjoy the local festivals, parties and events that are hosted throughout the parish all year round as well as thirty to forty something couples holding up for a weekend getaway. Multi generations renting the entire villa for a family retreat to the cool countryside.


WHATS NEARBY

Rafting on the Rio Grande is a fantastic way to spend a day: you are driven high up the Blue Mountains to join the river and spend the next five or six hours travelling downstream on a banana raft, stopping to eat and swim in the clear river water. One of the more agreeable waterfalls, Reach Falls past Manchioneal to the east of Port Antonio, and Somerset Falls to its west, near Hope Bay. One of the finest beaches in the world, Frenchman's Cove, four miles past the Trident. A cove into which the freshwater streams also run, overhung with tropical foliage. Winnifred Beach, ia also a couple of miles on at Boston Bay, is also exceptional. When clean, the Norwich Beach, to the west of Port Antonio, is one of the unsung gems of the Caribbean.
